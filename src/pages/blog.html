My Journey into Software Development

Posted on August 24, 2024

Software development has always fascinated me, but it wasn’t until I joined the University of Nairobi that I fully immersed myself in the world of coding. At first, I was overwhelmed by the vast number of programming languages and frameworks out there. However, I soon realized that learning to code is more about problem-solving and less about memorizing syntax. I started with Python, which was incredibly beginner-friendly. The clear syntax and readability allowed me to focus on understanding algorithms and data structures without getting bogged down by complex code. From there, I ventured into web development, experimenting with HTML, CSS, and JavaScript. Recently, I have been diving deeper into React, a popular JavaScript library for building user interfaces. Despite being a beginner, I am excited about the endless possibilities that React offers for creating dynamic and responsive web applications. This blog will document my journey through software development, sharing the challenges, successes, and everything in between. I hope that my experiences can serve as a resource and inspiration for anyone looking to embark on a similar path.

Balancing Economics and Software Development

Posted on August 18, 2024

Studying economics while pursuing a passion for software development has been an interesting balancing act. As an economics student, I am trained to think analytically and understand complex systems—skills that are surprisingly applicable to coding. One of the most significant overlaps I’ve found is in the area of data analysis. Economics often involves analyzing large datasets to identify trends and make predictions. Similarly, in software development, particularly in fields like machine learning and data science, analyzing and interpreting data is crucial. My knowledge of economics has given me a unique perspective on how data can be leveraged to build smarter algorithms and applications. However, balancing these two fields is not without its challenges. There are times when my workload is overwhelming, especially when assignments and project deadlines overlap. To manage this, I’ve learned the importance of time management and prioritization. I try to stay organized by maintaining a detailed schedule and setting clear, achievable goals. Ultimately, I believe that combining these two disciplines will allow me to create innovative solutions that have a real-world impact. Whether it’s developing software that can analyze economic data or building financial models that can predict market trends, I am excited about the possibilities that lie ahead.

My Love for Classical Music and How It Influences My Work

Posted on August 5, 2024

Music has always been a big part of my life, especially classical music. The complex compositions of Beethoven, the soothing melodies of Chopin, and the dynamic rhythms of Stravinsky have been my constant companions during late-night coding sessions and study marathons. I find that classical music helps me concentrate and stay focused, particularly when I’m working on challenging coding problems or studying dense economic theories. The structured yet intricate nature of classical pieces mirrors the logical and sometimes complex nature of programming. There’s something about the harmony and precision in classical music that resonates with the logical mindset needed for coding. Listening to classical music while working also boosts my creativity. It encourages me to think outside the box and explore different approaches to solving problems. I often find myself drawing parallels between composing music and writing code; both require a deep understanding of structure, a flair for creativity, and an eye for detail. In this blog, I plan to explore the connection between music and technology further. I’ll share playlists that help me focus, dive into the science of music and productivity, and maybe even discuss how algorithms are used in music composition. Stay tuned!
